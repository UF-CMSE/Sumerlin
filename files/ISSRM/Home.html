Welcome

The International Symposium on Stimuli-Responsive Materials (ISSRM) is a non-profit consortium gathering scientists and engineers interested in the design, synthesis, characterization, and understanding of the physical, chemical, and applied principles of stimuli-responsive materials and devices.


Having began in 2003 at the University of Southern Mississippi with partial support from the National Science Foundation, the ISSRM has developed into one of the best annual opportunities for academic, industrial, and government researchers at all levels to discuss the the most recent advances in the areas of responsive and adaptive materials. Presentation themes range from smart materials, polymer synthesis, responsive biological materials, biomimicry, engineering of shape-adaptive objects, nanomaterials design, and theoretical predictions/simulations.


Plenary speakers deliver presentations on recent advances in their respective fields of expertise, and evening poster sessions provide a forum for scientific exchange, fruitful discussions, and networking. On behalf of the Scientific Advisory Board, we invite you to participate in the next symposium to be held in Santa Rosa, CA, in the heart of Sonoma wine country.


You may view abstracts of past meetings on this website, and for further information, contact the Symposium Chairs or members of the Scientific Advisory Board. 

Now in its seventh year, the International Symposium on Stimuli-Responsive Materials will be held in Santa Rosa, CA, the heart of Sonoma wine country.
